Poverty in America


The Gender Wage Gap's Impact on Poverty

Goal


Understand and Voice the increased poverty and hardship caused by the gender wage gap.


Quick Facts


  • Woman’s paycheck averaging 81.5 cents to every man’s dollar.

  • If working women were given equal wages to men, their poverty rate would fall from 8.2% to 4.0%.

  • This would also decrease the rate of children with working mothers living in poverty from 5.6 million to 3.1 million.

  • The United States economy would have produced income of $512.6 billion more if women received equal pay; this represents 2.8 percent of 2016 gross domestic product (GDP).

    Purpose


    We decided on this topic of research due to our mutual interest in trying to help people in need. The first thing that came to mind when we were first talking about people and helping our communities was poverty. Our original project idea was a comparison between urban and rural poverty, but we could not find the right set of data for the research. However, we still liked the idea of contrast in poverty, so we wondered how the wage gap impacted poverty. With working women and single mothers making up a large population of America in poverty, we wanted to know how much the gender wage gap impacts families close to the poverty line.
